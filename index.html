Literature Survey

A comprehensive review of existing research and tools in AI-driven health management

üîé

Current State of Research

Our literature survey reviewed more than 50 scholarly articles and industry studies published between 2015‚Äì2025 on AI-driven health management, fitness adherence, and predictive analytics. Several core themes were identified, including the rise of machine learning in personalized healthcare, the use of neural networks for health risk prediction, and the integration of conversational AI for continuous user engagement and motivation.

The survey revealed that while numerous digital health platforms support basic fitness tracking and monitoring, few systems effectively combine predictive modeling, personalized intervention planning, and intelligent coaching within a single integrated framework. Most existing solutions emphasize data collection and visualization rather than delivering adaptive, real-time, and personalized health decision support.

üìä

Key Findings (Sri Lankan Context)

  • 75% of Sri Lankan adults face NCD risks due to poor lifestyle management and limited preventive digital tools.
  • Existing local fitness systems lack integration with predictive analytics and personalized health planning.
  • The Health Guardian model achieved 84% accuracy in localized health risk prediction.
  • AI-driven personalized fitness plans improved user adherence among Sri Lankan adults.
  • Conversational AI increased engagement, aligning with rising smartphone use in Sri Lanka.
  • The research highlights the need for culturally adapted, affordable, and privacy-compliant digital health solutions.

Research Gap

Identified limitations in current AI-driven health management systems

üîç

Identified Gaps

  • Integration Gap: Lack of an integrated platform that combines fitness tracking, health risk prediction, and personalized intervention within a single system.
  • Localization Gap: Absence of localized and culturally relevant digital health models tailored to Sri Lankan users and healthcare contexts.
  • Adaptive Learning Gap: Limited use of real-time adaptive machine learning for continuously updating health recommendations based on user behavior and physiological changes.
  • Engagement Gap: Insufficient integration of AI-driven conversational coaching to sustain long-term user motivation and adherence in preventive health management.

These gaps present a significant opportunity for our research to make a novel contribution to the field by developing an approach that addresses these limitations.

Primary Research Questions

Key research questions driving our investigation into AI-driven health management

‚ùì

Research Questions

  • Health Risk Prediction: How can Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) be applied to accurately predict personalized health risk levels among Sri Lankan adults?
  • Activity Monitoring: What computational methods can dynamically monitor and analyze user activity levels to enhance fitness adherence?
  • Insurance Personalization: How can AI-driven recommendation systems personalize insurance plans based on individual health risks and lifestyle patterns?
  • Conversational AI Integration: How can conversational AI be integrated to provide personalized health coaching that improves motivation and long-term engagement?

These research questions guide our investigation into developing an integrated AI-powered health management system that addresses the unique needs of Sri Lankan adults.

Project Objectives

The specific goals we aim to achieve through this research

üéØ

Main Objective

To enhance adult health management by analyzing near real-time fitness data using an Adaptive Activity Scoring Algorithm, Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) for risk prediction and customized insurance plans, and Random Forest Specifier with Grid Search for exercise and health recommendations, with the goal of promoting proactive health monitoring and wellness optimization.

üìã

Specific Objectives

  • Fitness System for Activity Level Calculation and Goal Monitoring: To develop a fitness system that accurately calculate activity levels, monitor progress, and provides personalized insights to enhance user engagement, consistency, and overall health outcomes.
  • Predict Risk and Identify Trends At Members: To analyze health data and identify at-risk individuals by predicting health trends and providing timely interventions to improve overall compliance and health levels.
  • Personalized Exercise & Health Recommendation System: To develop adaptive and personalized intervention plans that dynamically adjust based on individuals' health data, risk levels, and exercise preferences, promoting sustainable behavior changes and improved long-term health outcomes for corporate employees.
  • Smart Insurance Plan Recommendation: To Develop a AI system that first checks insurance eligibility using a rule-based approach, then evaluates eligible individuals based on risk level. The system will recommend a personalized insurance plan tailored to each individual's profile.

Research Methodology

Our comprehensive approach to developing and validating the intelligent health management system

üî¨

Mixed Methods Approach

Our research employs a mixed-methods approach combining qualitative and quantitative techniques to design and validate an intelligent health management system integrating activity level calculation, risk prediction, smart insurance planning, and personalized exercise generation.

1

Exploratory Research

  • Literature review of fuzzy logic, interpolation, rule-based systems, neural networks, and ensemble learning methods
  • Expert consultations to identify key health indicators and decision factors
  • Collection and preprocessing of real-world fitness and health data for model development
2

Methodology Development

  • Design of a hybrid framework combining multiple analytical and AI-based methods
  • Fuzzy Logic Classification for uncertainty handling in user inputs
  • Linear Interpolation Method for refining and smoothing fuzzy outputs
  • Rule-Based Classification for transparent and domain-driven decision-making
  • Artificial Neural Network (ANN) for predicting individual health risk levels
  • Random Forest Algorithm for activity level estimation, exercise recommendation, and insurance plan optimization
3

Tool Implementation

  • Development of a prototype system integrating all predictive and analytical modules
  • Implementation of the hybrid model architecture within a unified software platform
  • User interface designed for real-time insights, recommendations, and result visualization
4

Validation and Evaluation

  • Controlled experiments with selected participants to test model accuracy and reliability
  • Comparative analysis of predicted results against expert assessments
  • Quantitative and qualitative evaluation of performance, usability, and interpretability
  • Refinement of the methodology and tool based on validation outcomes
